About

Get to know me

After graduating with honors from College as a Software Engineer at the age of 21, I began my career as a Jr .NET Developer at a small company based in Monterrey not really knowing what exactly I wanted to do for the rest of my life with software development being so vast. There, I built mostly Windows Forms and WPF CRUD applications using Entity Framework.

After 6 months passed, I felt ready for a bigger challenge (as well as the corporate world) and accepted an invitation to join Accenture's team. Unlucky for me, there were no .NET positions left , thus I spent some time getting exposure to Enterprise Java via in-house training for a few positions. However, I quickly noticed Java wasn't my thing (I wasn't a big fan of most Java Tooling's old school aesthetic).

As luck would have it, I was offered an opportunity to switch to Front End Development. I quickly accepted it thinking "it can't be worse than Java", but boy was wrong. Within the next couple of days, I grew increasingly in love with Web Development. I was marvelled at the jawdroppingly beatiful and complex web applicatons that could be built with only 3 seemingly simple languages: HTML, CSS & JavaScript.

As time went on, the more I developed for the web, the more I fell in love with it, and the more I decided this was what I wanted to do the rest of my life. My love for front end got me so far to even being switched into Avanade, Accenture and Microsoft's Digital and Interactive division in Monterrey that was a bigger powerhouse in Front End Development and Design. I also grew increasingly interested in web design and the UI/UX areas.

During my time at Avanade, I've had great opportunities to develop applications using technologies like; BackboneJS, AngularJS, Ionic Framework, Bootstrap/Foundation, and lately ReactJS and Webpack (I'm really excited about the last 2). However, as time went on, it became clear my drive for learning had made me quickly outgrow my position, and with no opportunities looming on the horizon, I decided to join the team at Inflection Point Systems in early 2016

Fast forward to today, and not much has changed. My passion for front end lies still strong, however, it's been made increasingly clear just knowing Front End just isn't enough in 2016, thus my new goal was born; Become the best Full Stack Web Developer out there.
